## Premise

Identity layers promise stable boundaries, but real systems continuously mutate. The more abstraction layers we add, the less visible those mutations become.

## Failure Signals

1. Policy objects grow faster than ownership boundaries.
2. Emergency exceptions are never retired.
3. Token trust duration exceeds attack discovery time.

### Practical Heuristic

When policy churn over 90 days outpaces service ownership changes by more than $2x$, expect authorization blind spots.

## Experimental Method

We built a longitudinal telemetry pipeline to compare IAM declaration state against runtime call-graphs.

## Findings

Most collapse events were not abrupt. They were preceded by gradual semantic drift in claims translation.

## Implications

The right objective is not perfect policy. The objective is early visibility into mismatch velocity.
